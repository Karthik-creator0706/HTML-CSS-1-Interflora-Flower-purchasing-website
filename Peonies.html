    Peonies and Peony Bouquets FAQs

    How long do peonies last in a bouquet?

    Typically, peony bouquets can last about five to seven days. As with most bouquets, making your peonies last is all about proper care. Here's how to make your peony bouquet last:

  • Before you place your bouquet in water, cut the stems at an angle.
  • Replace the water every two days.
  • Store your peonies in the fridge overnight.

    • Whether you plan your peony flowers delivery ahead or send them the same day, you can rest assured that you or your recipient will be pleased by the quality of our flowers.

    Are peonies good for wedding bouquets?

    When you want to send flowers as a wedding gift, peonies are a great choice. This is because, in addition to their beauty, they symbolize love and happiness. With their links to Greek mythology, they have grown to become synonymous with good fortune and prosperity. So, if you're considering a peony bouquet delivery, you can rest assured that they're the perfect flower to accompany you when saying your vows. And, these colorful flowers come in a broad spectrum of hues to perfectly match your theme. Whether you’ve dreamed of classic blushing pink flowers, romantic red peonies, or even a joyous yellow arrangement, peonies can bring your vision to life.

    When are peonies in season?

    People adore peonies because they bear enormous, magnificent blooms in spring and early summer. These intensely fragrant flowers bloom from late April until mid-June, but it is the month of May that peonies truly hold center stage.

    Tips for making peony bouquets last

    A peony delivery can last more than a week when properly cared for. In bud form, peonies can be kept alive for several months when placed in the fridge. Keep these tips in mind for keeping your peonies fresh and vibrant after delivery.

  • Remove any foliage that will be below the water line
  • Using sharp shears, cut the stems at a diagonal to allow for maximum water absorption
  • Put them in a tall vase with at least 5 inches of room temperature water
  • Add plant food to the water, or a spoonful of granulated sugar
  • Choose a cool place out of direct sunlight
  • Recut the stems every three days when replacing the water
